⚒ Modern Manufacturing & Curing Methods

1. The Role of Admixtures

Plasticizers allow the mix to flow into the molds and compact densely under vibration with very little water. However, they do not create the chemical reaction (hydration) that gives cement its strength. Water is still required for that.

2. Why Traditional Curing is Rare

Because production speed is high, submerging thousands of blocks in water tanks or constantly spraying them is impractical and can wash away the fine surface cement before it fully sets.

3. The "No-Water" Curing Solution

Instead of adding water, factories use Moisture Retention. Freshly demolded pallets are placed in humid chambers for 24 hours, then tightly wrapped in polythene plastic sheets for 7 to 14 days. This traps the existing moisture inside, allowing hydration to finish without external watering.
